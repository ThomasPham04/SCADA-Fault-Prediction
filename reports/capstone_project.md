The project focuses on applying Deep Learning models to predict industrial equipment faults based on data collected from SCADA systems. SCADA data contain various sensor signals, such as pressure, temperature, voltage, current, and flow rate, which can be exploited to predict failures early, prevent production interruptions, and reduce maintenance costs.

Theoretical review: characteristics of SCADA data and deep learning techniques, including RNN, LSTM, GRU, and CNN.

Data collection and preprocessing: data normalization, missing data detection, and extraction of important features.

Development of deep learning models:

LSTM/GRU for time-series data.

CNN or hybrid CNN-LSTM models for learning complex features.

Model training and evaluation: using real or simulated SCADA data and applying evaluation metrics such as Precision, Recall, F1-score, and RMSE.

Practical application: developing a prototype real-time fault prediction system integrated with an early warning module.
